## Section A — 90-second intro (0:00 – 1:30)

> Hold on a static frame of the Wall's Ch1 hero ("What's standing
> between you and a job?") for the first 5 seconds before voiceover
> begins. Let it land.

**[0:00 — 0:05]** *(silent — Ch1 hero on screen)*

**[0:05 — 0:25]** *(20s — the hook)*
"What if the path through the wall was visible? Not the jobs — the
*wall*. The five offices, the forty-seven forms, the bus that takes
seventy-one minutes when the schedule says forty-five, the credit
score that closes doors you never knocked on."

**[0:25 — 0:50]** *(25s — what GoWork is)*
"GoWork is a workforce navigator. It maps the wall — every barrier
between a worker and a paycheck — and routes them around it. It does
the math, it sequences the path, it hands them the plan. Where job
boards tell you what jobs exist, GoWork tells you why you can't get
one — and then helps you fix it."

**[0:50 — 1:15]** *(25s — what we're about to show)*
"We're going to walk through one resident's twelve weeks in Fort
Worth, Texas. Carlos is twenty-nine. A father of one. Three years past
a misdemeanor, sentence complete. Four years of warehouse work behind
him, a forklift cert in his pocket. He has three hundred dollars and
no car. He is ready to work. Watch the cliff math, watch the avatar's
path, watch the constellation of barriers."

**[1:15 — 1:30]** *(15s — the bridge)*
"This is The Wall."

> *(Ch1 → Ch2 transition begins. Camera dive starts on the audio
> punctuation of "Wall.")*

## Section B — 3-minute walkthrough (1:30 – 4:30)

> Each chapter gets 15–25 seconds of voiceover. Narration timing
> matches the chapter timing in `docs/submission-demo.md` so the
> voiceover and the on-screen scroll stay in sync.

### Ch1 — Continental (already on screen at 1:30 — bridge from intro)

> No additional voiceover. The intro ended on "This is The Wall," and
> the Ch1 hero question is held silently for two beats before the
> camera dives.

### Ch2 — City Arrival (1:30 – 1:50, ~20s)

"Fort Worth, Texas. Carlos lives east of downtown — past Sundance
Square, past the courthouse. ZIP seven-six-one-one-nine. Trinity
Metro routes thread the city in cyan. Half of them don't reach the
warehouses where the work is."

### Ch3 — The Neighborhood (1:50 – 2:10, ~20s)

"Carlos is twenty-nine. Single father of one daughter, four years
old. Three years past a misdemeanor charge — sentence complete. Four
years of warehouse work, forklift certified. Three hundred dollars,
no vehicle, four barriers."

### Ch4 — The Wall (2:10 – 2:50, ~40s)

"Most workforce sites pretend barriers don't matter. We do the math
to prove they do. Criminal record: the Tarrant County District Clerk
is four-point-eight miles away — Bus Four plus Bus Six totals
seventy-one minutes. No transit: forty-five minutes is the schedule;
eighty-seven is the day. No childcare: twelve hundred dollars a month
without a subsidy. Bad credit: a five-forty score, late payments,
collections — one in three job listings is unreachable."

### Ch5 — The Labyrinth (2:50 – 3:20, ~30s)

"Five offices. Forty-seven forms. Each one says go to the next one.
Without GoWork, this is the path. We named it so we could replace
it."

### Ch6 — The Math (3:20 – 4:00, ~40s)

"When more pay means less money. Carlos stocks shelves at Amazon
DFW5 — seventy-one minutes from home on Bus Four. A two-dollar raise
sounds like a win. The benefits cliff says otherwise: childcare
subsidy gone at fourteen an hour. SNAP gone at seventeen. We do the
cliff math so the choice is honest."

### Ch7 — The Path (4:00 – 4:50, ~50s)

"Five stops. Twelve weeks. One plan. Home to DPS to HHSC to Legal
Aid to Workforce Solutions. Trinity Metro Bus Four and Bus Six carry
him between them. We named the path so we could walk it with him."

### Ch8 — The Graph (4:50 – 5:40, ~50s)

"The wall isn't a list. It's a graph. Thirty-three nodes. Seven
categories. Every barrier connects to two more — resolve one, and
three others move within reach. The constellation breathes above
downtown Fort Worth. We mapped the wall so we could route around
it."

### Ch9 — Any City (5:40 – 6:10, ~30s)

"It works in Fort Worth. It will work in Montgomery. It will work
where you are. Two cities deployed. Six on deck. Same engine, same
playbook, local data."

### Ch10 — Find Your Path (6:10 – 6:30, ~20s)

> Navigation timing condensed in the script — the actual video
> compresses these to fit the 3-minute walkthrough budget; absolute
> timestamps in Section D's master timeline are the source of truth.

"You've seen the wall. You've seen the labyrinth. Now skip both —
start your assessment, and we'll hand you a plan that's already done
the offices, forms, and math."

## Section F — Recording notes (for the demo-runner)

- **Read at human pace.** No compression artifacts, no faster-than-
  conversational read. The Wall doesn't need urgency — the prose
  carries it.
- **Punctuate the bridge.** "This is The Wall." is the 1:30 cue. The
  Ch1 → Ch2 camera dive must start on the period.
- **Don't talk over the slider drag.** Ch6 voiceover ends before the
  drag begins. The drag is its own visual beat.
- **Don't talk over the Ch8 reveal.** The 3D barrier graph rising is
  the wow moment — the voiceover ends 2 seconds before the camera
  tilt-up so the visual lands silent.
- **Hold on the Ch10 CTA for the full outro.** No more scrolling
  after "skip both — start your assessment."
- **Caption parity:** the SRT at `docs/submission-video.srt`
  transcribes this script verbatim. If the voiceover deviates,
  re-export captions before publishing.
